ABOUT ME

Diana Hajali is currently an International Trade Specialist at the Department of Commerce's International Trade Administration, where she works on standards-related trade barriers in the Office of Standards and Intellectual Property. Her portfolio includes the Middle East and North Africa region and the European Union. Prior to ITA, Diana graduated from the Bush School of Government and Public Service with a master's degree in international affairs. Diana also has a bachelor's degree in international studies focusing on politics and diplomacy, and a minor in French, from Texas A&M University.


Diana has traveled to various countries around the world, stayed in a refugee camp, and completed a study abroad program in Grenoble, France. She is also a Robertson Foundation for Government alum and currently serves on the Alumni Council. Additionally, she serves as an Alumni Ambassador for her former study abroad program, Cultural Experiences Aborad (CEA). Diana knows advanced Arabic and French.


Some of Diana's skills include public speaking, which she enhanced during her time as president of the Texas A&M Speech and Debate Team, communication, research, and leadership. Diana has a passion for international trade, cross-cultural exchange programs, immigration, and study abroad. In her free time, Diana enjoys reading, exercising, and playing the piano or video games.
